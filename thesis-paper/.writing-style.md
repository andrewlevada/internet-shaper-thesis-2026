# Writing style notes

Target: clear, direct academic prose. Not stiff, not casual.

- Sentences are concise. One idea per sentence, broken by a comma when needed — not chained clauses.
- No fussy or theatrical phrasing. "This is incompatible with X" beats "This approach is fundamentally at odds with the problem at hand, where the conflict lies precisely between..."
- Fix broken structure and typos, but don't bloat the sentence with extra words to sound formal.
- Replace made-up hyphenated jargon (e.g. "end-user-controled") with plain readable phrasing ("controlled by the end user").
- Keep "we" — it reads naturally in CS papers even for single authors.
- Acronyms spelled out on first use, then abbreviated.
- Rhetorical questions converted to statements.
- Content inside parentheses preserved exactly as written, including typos and dashes.
- Empty/stub sections skipped.
- Russian text in user study left as-is.
- Lists follow the thesis guide: full-sentence items get capital start and period end.
